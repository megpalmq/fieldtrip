General Information

The Bandolier Bag is a traditional Native American artifact, often made from leather and adorned with intricate beadwork. It was used to carry items and is a symbol of cultural identity.

The bag is typically worn across the body and features a wide strap for comfort. The designs often hold significant meaning, reflecting the wearer's heritage and beliefs.

Artist: Unrecorded Ho-Chunk artist

Culture: Ho-Chunk

Date: 1895-1915

Medium: wool cloth with loom woven glass seed beads, wool yarn, silk ribbon

Artist: Unrecorded Potawatomi artist

Culture: Potawatomi

Date: 1880

Medium: wool and cotton cloth, loom woven glass beads on cotton thread, wool yarn, wool fox braid, printed cotton lining and pouch interior
